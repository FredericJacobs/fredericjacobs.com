On SMS Logins II : an example from Telegram in Russia

Apr 29, 2016

A few months ago, I wrote a post on SMS logins following a wave of attacks attempting to take over Iranian Telegram accounts. Recently, there have been new confirmed cases of attacks in both Iran and Russia.

Oleg Kozlovsky is a leader of the Oborona civic youth movement in Russia. The movement is known for opposing an authoritarian vector of Russian policy and supporting nonviolent resistance to the authorities. At 2:25AM on April 29th Moscow-time, he [explain how he knew he got disconnected then]. His, carrier MTS, Russia’s largest mobile operator. After 15 minutes of disconnection, someone tries to log into Oleg’s account using a Telegram command-line interface over the Tor network. The process for authorizing a device to a Telegram accounts simply consists of entering a verification code received by SMS. Oleg doesn’t receive the message, but at 3:08AM the attacker enters the authorization code and gets access to Oleg’s account. Telegram displayed a notification about the newly logged in device, but Oleg was sleeping and only saw it in the morning. [EXPLAIN Cell phone coverage coming back.]

Four minutes later, the same Tor exit node IP address, logs into George Alburov’s Telegram account.

With Telegram versed in how there was now hacking my account at night and probably George Alburi. It’s an interesting story. Events unfolded as follows: At 2:25 the night of MTS technical security department disables me delivery service of SMS-messages. After 15 minutes, at 2:40, someone with the Unix-console by IP-address 162.247.72.27 (this is one of the Tor anonymizer servers) Telegram sent a request for authorization of a new device with my phone number. I sent an SMS with a code that has not been delivered (the service is disabled for me). 3:08 The attacker enters the authorization code and get access to my account. Telegram sent me an automatic notification about this (which I read in the morning). 3:12 In the same way from the same IP-address (ie through the same session Tor) cracked account Jora Alburova. At 4:55 of technical security department MTS again includes me delivery service SMS. The reason for opening and closing the MTS service called I refused, offering to write a letter of inquiry. The main question is how the unknown persons had access to the code, which was sent to the SMS, but not delivered. Unfortunately, I have only one version: through SORM system or directly through the MTS tehbezopasnosti department (for example, on a call from the “competent authorities”). If there are other options - offer. The main recommendation for all users Telegram: connect the two-stage authorization (ie not only SMS, but also the password). This is done in the security settings. The main recommendation for the Telegram: not accept the authorization code, if it is not proof of delivery.
